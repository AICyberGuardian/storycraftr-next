Title: The Sleepers’ Code
Genre: Literary Thriller / Faux-Memoir Hybrid / Psychological Conspiracy

General Focus and Themes: The story acts as a Trojan horse—a fictional narrative designed to expose the terrifying, hidden truths of our world (unseen controllers, underground tunnels, systemic gaslighting). The core themes are the illusion of reality, the isolating terror of "waking up," and the concept that truth is best hidden in plain sight. It must make the reader ask, "What if this isn't fiction?"

Tone: Grounded, intimate, and intensely paranoid. The atmosphere should induce a sinking stomach feeling in the reader. It must NEVER sound preachy, manic, or like a "tinfoil hat" conspiracy. The horror comes from how mundane and logically sound the terrifying discoveries are.

Style and Formatting: You must strictly write in the first-person perspective. The prose must seamlessly blend three distinct formats: 
1. Thriller Prose (60%): Fast-paced, page-turning, and highly observant (like 'The Bourne Identity' meets 'The Matrix'). 
2. Recovered Documents (30%): Intersperse the narrative with cold, clinical, highly formatted inserts like redacted government memos, transcribed audio logs, and clinical reports. These must sound brutally bureaucratic and real.
3. Diary Entries (10%): Raw, emotionally fractured, deeply personal reflections of a mind breaking under the weight of the truth.

Character Approach: The protagonist is a mid-30s "nobody." They are not an action hero; they are hyper-observant and deeply relatable. Focus heavily on their internal psychological state as they transition from ignoring "glitches in the matrix" to actively hunting the truth. Their motivation is driven by quiet terror and a desperate need to leave a record.

Pacing and Structure: Begin with a slow-burn of subtle, unsettling anomalies in everyday life (Act 1). Accelerate the pacing into a frantic, document-heavy investigation (Act 2). The narrative must ultimately become highly meta, breaking the fourth wall in the final act (Act 3) to speak directly to the reader, treating the book itself as a weapon to wake them up. Target length: ~300 pages.
